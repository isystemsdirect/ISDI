# UG-UIX Stem Protocol

**Version:** v0.1.0

## Classification
Governed UIX upgrade and canon-promotion protocol

## Purpose
Establish a controlled pathway for extracting, naming, testing, versioning, promoting, deprecating, and governing elegant UI transitional elements, mouse/pointer elements, motion behaviors, surface treatments, attention cues, and future premium interface primitives within the UG-UIX doctrine.

## Core Principle
Do not canonize effects merely because they look elegant. Canonize the semantic UIX behavior, operational value, accessibility posture, performance discipline, and governance boundary behind the effect.

## Canon Line
- **UG-UIX Doctrine** is the stable law of the interface.
- **UG-UIX Stem Protocol** is the controlled evolution path.
- Elegant elements begin as stems, not instant canon.
- Partner-specific styling may inspire stems but does not become ecosystem canon without abstraction.
- Human clarity, accessibility, truth-state visibility, and authority discipline outrank visual flourish.

## Lifecycle Stages
1. **Seed**: Observed elegant behavior from deck, prototype, mockup, user interaction, or experimental surface.
2. **Stem**: Named and bounded candidate UIX behavior.
3. **Branch**: Reusable implementation appears in multiple compatible surfaces.
4. **Canon**: Accepted UG-UIX doctrine element.
5. **Legacy**: Deprecated, replaced, restricted, or superseded behavior.
